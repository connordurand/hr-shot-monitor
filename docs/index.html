Architecture

Two streams. One clock. Instant feedback.

The dashboard ingests beat-by-beat heart rate from Polar H-10 sensors over Bluetooth and audio shot events from a microphone — both synchronized to a shared system clock for precise, frame-accurate alignment.

❤️

Polar H-10 Heart Rate

Beat-by-beat HR captured via Bluetooth Low Energy. RR intervals are converted to instantaneous HR (HRinst = 60,000 / RRms) for sub-second physiological resolution.

🔊

Audio Shot Detection

Continuous microphone input computes RMS amplitude per frame. Frames exceeding a configurable dB threshold (default 70 dB) are flagged as potential shot events and timestamped.

⏱️

Synchronized Timeline

Both streams share one system clock, enabling precise mapping of physiological state to each shot — critical given the rapid succession of targets in skeet and trap.

🎯

Live Hit / Miss Labels

After each detected shot, an observer marks hit or miss via the dashboard. The label is appended to the synchronized event record, enabling real-time and post-session analysis.

📊

Real-Time Analytics

Cumulative hit rate, HR distributions across hits vs. misses, and a shot timeline update live — enabling in-session coaching decisions without any post-hoc processing.

💾

CSV Export

Full session data exports to CSV for downstream analysis. Lays groundwork for fusing LOE 2 shot-level data with longitudinal Oura biometrics into a unified adaptive coaching platform.

Research Findings

What the data revealed

Six weeks of Oura Ring data from eight USMA Skeet & Trap Team members showed consistent physiological signals tied to week-to-week performance changes.

  • Sleep latency was the strongest within-person predictor (r = −0.43, p = .02). Weeks with longer sleep onset correlated with lower scores relative to each shooter's own baseline.

  • Five biometric features — HRV balance, high-stress time, average heart rate, vascular age, and sleep latency — together explained 53% of the variance in centered shooting scores (OLS, R² = 0.53).

  • Within-person centering (ICC ≈ 0.36) was essential: raw cross-shooter comparisons are dominated by baseline talent, not physiological fluctuation.

  • The live dashboard captured the expected HR spike before shot break → parasympathetic recovery pattern, the physiological signature of a well-executed shot routine.
